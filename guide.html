Problem Description

Kitchen sinks commonly get blocked due to grease, food particles, and debris buildup. This causes slow drainage and can lead to unpleasant odors and potential water damage if not addressed promptly.

warning

Safety First

Never use chemical drain cleaners with a garbage disposal. Always turn off power to disposal units before working.

What You'll Need

Tools

  • Rubber gloves
  • Plunger (sink plunger)
  • Drain snake or wire coat hanger
  • Bucket

Materials

  • Baking soda (1 cup)
  • White vinegar (1 cup)
  • Hot water
  • Dish soap

Step-by-Step Solution

1 Remove Visible Debris expand_more

Put on rubber gloves and remove any visible food particles or debris from the drain opening. Use your hands or tweezers to pull out hair, food scraps, or other blockages you can see.

lightbulb

Tip: Keep a small strainer in your sink to catch debris in the future.

2 Try Hot Water Flush expand_more

Boil a large pot of water and carefully pour it down the drain in 2-3 stages, allowing the hot water to work for several seconds between pours. This can dissolve grease and soap buildup.

warning

Caution: Don't use boiling water on PVC pipes - use very hot tap water instead.

3 Baking Soda & Vinegar Treatment expand_more

Pour 1 cup of baking soda down the drain, followed by 1 cup of white vinegar. Cover the drain with a plug or wet cloth and let it sit for 15-30 minutes. The chemical reaction will help break down organic matter.

After waiting, flush with hot water to clear the loosened debris.

4 Use a Plunger expand_more

If the drain is still slow, use a sink plunger (smaller than a toilet plunger). Fill the sink with enough water to cover the plunger cup, then plunge vigorously 15-20 times.

lightbulb

Tip: Block the overflow hole with a wet cloth for better suction.

5 Snake the Drain expand_more

If other methods fail, use a drain snake or straightened wire coat hanger to physically remove the blockage. Insert it into the drain and twist while pushing to catch debris, then pull it out.

Repeat this process several times, then flush with hot water.

Prevention Tips

restaurant

Avoid Grease

Never pour cooking grease or oil down the drain. Let it solidify and throw it in the trash.

filter_alt

Use Strainers

Install sink strainers to catch food particles before they go down the drain.

hot_tub

Weekly Flush

Run hot water down the drain weekly to prevent buildup of soap and grease.

cleaning_services

Regular Cleaning

Clean your sink strainer regularly and scrape plates before washing.
